Deep Learning-Based Sequence Optimization of a PET-Degrading Enzyme

Introduction

Plastic pollution is a growing environmental challenge, with polyethylene terephthalate (PET) making up nearly 10% of all plastic waste. PETase, an enzyme from Ideonella sakaiensis, has the potential to break down PET into its building blocks, enabling efficient recycling. However, its natural form is not optimized for industrial applications. My research focuses on leveraging machine learning to redesign PETase, enhancing its stability and catalytic efficiency for real-world use.

Machine Learning and Computational Methods

  • ProteinMPNN: A deep learning model trained for sequence design, used to generate optimized PETase variants.
  • HMM-based Sequence Search (HHblits): Employed for iterative sequence searching, helping refine candidate enzyme sequences.
  • AlphaFold: Used for structure prediction, evaluating the folding stability and structural integrity of redesigned sequences.
  • TemStaPro & ProteinSol: Applied to assess the thermostability and solubility of modified PETase variants.

Results and Impact

By combining these computational techniques, we generated high-confidence enzyme designs that exhibit improved structural properties. The next step is to experimentally validate these optimized PETase variants using yeast surface display and functional assays. Ultimately, this work demonstrates how machine learning can accelerate enzyme engineering, offering innovative solutions for plastic degradation and sustainability.
